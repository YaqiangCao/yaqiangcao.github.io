Brief Research Biograph

Yaqiang was enrolled in the major of bioengineering for college, before which he nearly knew nothing about the computer. He lost interest in making fermenters during his second/third year in college and found mathematical modeling and coding were quite full of fun when he participated in some competitions such as CUMCM. Then he found the field of bioinformatics/computational biology.

Yaqiang got his Ph.D. in Computational Biology from CAS-MPG Partner Institute for Computational Biology, Shanghai Institutes for Biological Sciences, University of Chinese Academy of Sciences under the supervision of Dr. Jing-Dong Jackie Han. He is now a bioinformatics Postdoc in NHLBI/NIH, under the mentoring of Dr. Keji Zhao.

Yaqiang has more than ten years of experience applying and analyzing deep sequencing technologies (RNA-Seq, ChIP-Seq, DNase-Seq, ATAC-seq, Hi-C, etc.) to understand transcription regulation during multiple biological processes of molecular systems. He focused on the epigenetic roles of human transposable elements during his Ph.D. student studies. Yaqiang is now working on understanding the chromatin interaction landscapes of human and mouse immune cells, especially on T cells. Collaborating closely with wet-lab mates, he analyzes and integrates sequencing data to intercept biology insights, and develops the analysis methods for new chromatin 1D and 3D profiling sequencing protocols.

Yaqiang’s initial research interests include data mining, machine learning from massive sequencing data, and bioinformatics tools development rooted in Dr. Han’s lab. His interests turned to sequencing-based methodologies development and T-cells immunology during the Postdoc training with Dr. Zhao.

Yaqiang has published the first/co-first author’s research articles on Nucleic Acids Research (2022 and 2020), Blood (2020), Bioinformatics (2020), and Genome Research (2019); many other solid interesting research papers as contributing collaborator such as Science (2022), Immunity (2022a&b), Nature Cell Biology (2022), Nature Metabolism (2020), Science Advances (2020), Cell Reports (2019), Cell Research (2015) and so on.

Yaqiang is also an active reviewer for Frontiers in Immunology, Frontiers in Oncology, Frontiers in Pharmacology, Frontiers In Genetics and BMC Genomics, happy to serve the community.

Outside the working time, Yaqiang is interested in the research topics he is not involved in yet (not limited to biology). Diversity of knowledge is fantastic.
