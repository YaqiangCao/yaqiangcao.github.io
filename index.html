Brief Research Biography

Yaqiang started his college education in bioengineering, but discovered his passion for mathematical modeling and coding through competitions such as CUMCM. This led him to computational biology, where he earned his PhD from the CAS-MPG Partner Institute for Computational Biology under Dr. Jing-Dong Jackie Han. Currently, he works as a bioinformatics postdoc with Dr.Keji Zhao at the NHLBI/NIH, focusing on the chromatin interaction landscapes of human and mouse immune cells, particularly T cells.

With over ten years of experience in deep sequencing (RNA-seq, ChIP-seq, DNase-seq, ATAC-seq, MNase-seq, Hi-C, Hi-TrAC, STARR-seq, 10X scRNA-seq, etc.), Yaqiang studies transcription regulation across biological processes. During his Ph.D., he focused on the epigenetic roles of human transposable elements; now, he explores chromatin interaction patterns in T cells. By collaborating closely with wet-lab colleagues, he integrates and analyzes sequencing data for biological insights, developing cutting-edge methods for new chromatin 1D and 3D profiling protocols.

Previously, Yaqiang focused on data mining and machine learning from large-scale sequencing data while in Dr. Han’s lab. His postdoctoral training under Dr. Zhao emphasized sequencing-based methodologies and T-cell immunology. As first or co-first author, he has published in Immunity (2024 & 2023[cover]), Nature Cell Biology (2024), Nucleic Acids Research (2023, 2022, 2020), Nature Communications (2022), Blood (2020[Plenary Paper]), Bioinformatics (2020), and Genome Research (2019), and has contributed to other impactful research papers in Science (2022), Immunity (2022a,b), Nature Cell Biology (2022), Nature Metabolism (2020), Science Advances (2020), Cell Reports (2019), and Cell Research (2015).

Yaqiang is a reviewer for several journals (Bioinformatics, Communication Biology, BMC Genomics, F1000Research, and more) and is dedicated to serving the scientific community. He actively develops and maintains open-source bioinformatics software (cLoops, cLoops2, astroBoy) recognized by multiple labs.

Beyond research, Yaqiang enjoys exploring sports and spending time with family.
